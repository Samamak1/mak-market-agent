Sector Rotation Basics
Sector rotation is the movement of investor money between industry groups as the economic cycle shifts. Early-cycle recoveries tend to favor financials, industrials and consumer discretionary names, while late-cycle and recessionary phases push money toward defensives such as utilities, healthcare and consumer staples. Rising rates often support banks and pressure long-duration technology stocks, while falling energy prices squeeze the energy sector directly. Traders watch relative strength between sector ETFs to see which groups are leading or lagging the broad index. Rotation matters because an index can look calm while violent moves happen underneath as money leaves one sector for another.
