Reading Options Flow
Options flow refers to the stream of option trades hitting exchanges, and many traders scan it for unusually large or aggressive orders. A large call sweep executed at the ask across multiple exchanges suggests urgency and is often read as a bullish bet, while heavy put buying can signal hedging or bearish positioning. Open interest shows how many contracts remain outstanding at each strike, and clusters of open interest can act like magnets near expiration. Flow is context dependent: an institution buying puts may simply be protecting a large stock position rather than predicting a decline. Volume without context is noise, so flow readers combine size, direction, aggressiveness and the underlying trend.
