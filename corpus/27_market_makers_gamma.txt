Market Makers and Gamma Effects
Options market makers take the other side of customer flow and hedge the resulting exposure by trading the underlying stock. The size of that hedge changes as price moves, an effect called gamma. When dealers are long gamma, their hedging leans against the market, buying dips and selling rips, which dampens volatility. When they are short gamma, hedging chases the market, selling into declines and buying into rallies, which amplifies moves. Around large option expirations these mechanical flows can dominate short-term price action. Estimating dealer positioning is inexact, but the framework explains why some tape days feel pinned and others feel unhinged.
