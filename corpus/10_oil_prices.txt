Oil Prices and Equities
Crude oil sits upstream of much of the economy, so its price feeds into inflation, transport costs, and the earnings of entire sectors. Rising oil lifts energy producers but taxes consumers and airlines, and a rapid spike can act like a brake on growth. Supply decisions by major producers, inventory reports, and geopolitical disruptions are the usual catalysts, while demand expectations swing with global growth forecasts. Traders distinguish between gradual price rises driven by healthy demand, which equities often absorb, and supply-shock spikes, which they usually do not. The relationship between oil and stocks is regime dependent rather than fixed.
