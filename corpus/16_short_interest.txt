Short Interest and Squeezes
Short interest is the number of shares sold short and not yet covered, often expressed as a percentage of float or as days-to-cover using average volume. High short interest signals widespread skepticism, but it also builds latent buying pressure, because every short position must eventually be bought back. When a heavily shorted stock rises quickly, shorts covering can accelerate the move into a squeeze. Borrow costs and utilization rates add color on how crowded the short side is. Squeezes are violent but usually temporary; positioning unwinds do not by themselves change a company's fundamentals.
