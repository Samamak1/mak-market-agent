What Happens on Fed Day
Eight times a year the Federal Open Market Committee meets and announces its policy rate decision at a scheduled time, followed by a press conference. Markets usually price the decision itself in advance, so attention falls on the statement's wording changes, the updated economic projections, and the dot plot showing where officials expect rates to go. The press conference often moves markets more than the statement as the chair fields questions and markets parse tone. Typical patterns include a quiet drift before the announcement, a sharp initial reaction, and a second reversal during the press conference. Positioning into Fed day is a risk decision in itself.
