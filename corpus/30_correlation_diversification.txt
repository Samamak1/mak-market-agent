Correlation and Real Diversification
Diversification only works when holdings do not move together, which is measured by correlation. Two positions with different tickers but the same driver, such as two rate-sensitive growth stocks, offer little real diversification. Correlations are unstable: they drift in calm markets and converge toward one in crises, exactly when protection is most needed, so a portfolio's risk should be judged under stressed correlations rather than average ones. Rolling correlation windows reveal how relationships change through time. Mixing genuinely different return drivers, across asset classes, geographies and factor exposures, is the practical route to a portfolio that survives regime changes.
