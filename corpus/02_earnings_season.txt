Earnings Season Mechanics
Four times a year, public companies report quarterly results, and the few weeks when most reports cluster are called earnings season. Analysts publish estimates for revenue and earnings per share in advance, and the market's reaction depends mostly on results versus those expectations rather than on absolute numbers. A company can grow profits and still sell off if it missed estimates or issued weak guidance. Guidance, management's forecast for coming quarters, often matters more than the reported quarter. Options markets price an expected move around each report, and realized moves that differ from that implied move drive gains and losses for volatility traders.
