Semiconductors as a Cyclical Tell
Semiconductors sit at the front of the technology supply chain, so orders for chips respond early to changes in demand for electronics, cars, servers and industrial equipment. That makes semiconductor indexes a favored leading indicator: relative strength in chips is read as confidence in future growth, while breakdowns warn of slowing orders. The industry is deeply cyclical, swinging between shortage and glut as capacity is added in large discrete steps. Inventory levels across the chain, book-to-bill ratios, and capital-equipment orders are the data points analysts monitor. Chip stocks amplify the market's growth expectations in both directions.
