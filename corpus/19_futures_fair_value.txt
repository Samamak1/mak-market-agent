Index Futures and Fair Value
Stock index futures trade nearly around the clock, so they carry price discovery while the cash market sleeps. Fair value is the theoretical futures price implied by the index level, interest rates, and expected dividends until expiration; pre-market commentary comparing futures to fair value is estimating the cash market open. Deviations are kept small by arbitrage between futures and the underlying basket. Overnight futures moves reflect global news flow, but thin overnight liquidity can exaggerate them, and gaps at the open often partially fill once full liquidity returns. Futures are also the standard tool for hedging equity exposure quickly.
