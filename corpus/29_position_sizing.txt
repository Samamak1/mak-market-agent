Position Sizing and Risk per Trade
Position sizing converts an idea into an amount of risk, and it does more for long-run results than entry timing. A common framework fixes the loss per trade at a small fraction of the account, often around one percent, then derives share size from the distance between entry and stop: size equals dollar risk divided by stop distance. Wider stops therefore force smaller positions, keeping every trade's worst case roughly equal. Sizing also caps aggregate exposure across correlated positions, since three trades in the same sector are closer to one large trade. Consistent sizing turns a strategy's edge into survivable, compoundable returns.
