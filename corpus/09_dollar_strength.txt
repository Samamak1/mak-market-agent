Why the Dollar Matters Everywhere
The US dollar is the world's reserve currency, so its strength or weakness ripples through every market. A strong dollar makes American exports more expensive, pressures the overseas earnings of multinationals when translated back, and tightens conditions for emerging markets that borrowed in dollars. Commodities priced in dollars, such as oil and gold, often move inversely to the currency. The dollar index, which measures the dollar against a basket of major currencies, is a standard gauge traders watch alongside rate differentials, since money tends to flow toward the currency offering higher real yields. Sharp dollar moves frequently precede or accompany stress elsewhere.
