Market Breadth: Looking Under the Hood
Market breadth measures how many stocks are participating in a move, rather than how far the index itself traveled. Common gauges include the advance-decline line, the share of stocks above their 200-day moving averages, and the count of new 52-week highs versus lows. An index grinding higher while breadth deteriorates means a shrinking group of large names is carrying the market, a divergence that has preceded some notable corrections. Broad participation, by contrast, is the signature of durable rallies. Breadth is a conditioning tool, useful for judging the quality of a trend rather than timing exact turns.
