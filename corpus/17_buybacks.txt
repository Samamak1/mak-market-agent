Share Buybacks and the Bid Underneath
When a company repurchases its own shares it reduces the share count, lifting earnings per share and returning cash to shareholders who sell. Buybacks have become one of the largest steady sources of equity demand, and blackout windows around earnings, when companies pause repurchases, subtly change the market's supply-demand balance. Critics argue buybacks can substitute for investment or be timed poorly at high prices; supporters see them as flexible capital return. For traders, the practical point is that large announced programs provide a persistent bid, while blackout periods remove it during the exact weeks earnings risk is highest.
