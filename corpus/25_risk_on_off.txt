Risk-On, Risk-Off Regimes
Markets often trade in regimes where assets move together based on appetite for risk rather than individual fundamentals. In risk-on phases, equities, credit, commodities and higher-yielding currencies rally while safe havens like government bonds, the yen and gold lag. Risk-off reverses the pattern within hours of a shock. Cross-asset correlation is the tell: when everything moves as one trade, single-name analysis matters less than the regime itself. Traders identify the regime through safe-haven currencies, bond yields, credit spreads and volatility gauges together. Regimes persist longer than expected, then flip abruptly, which is what makes them dangerous.
