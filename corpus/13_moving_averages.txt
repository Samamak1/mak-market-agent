Moving Averages in Practice
A moving average smooths price data over a lookback window, turning noisy candles into a single trend line. The 50-day and 200-day simple moving averages are widely watched on daily charts, and crossovers between them, like the golden cross and death cross, get heavy media attention despite mixed evidence as standalone signals. Traders more often use moving averages to define trend direction, to locate dynamic support and resistance, and to force discipline: above a rising average favors long setups, below a falling one favors caution. The choice of period matters less than applying whatever is chosen consistently.
