ETF Flows and What They Move
Exchange-traded funds now intermediate a huge share of equity trading, and their creation and redemption mechanics tie fund flows directly to the underlying stocks. Persistent inflows into an index ETF translate into systematic buying of every member, which some argue lifts correlations and favors the largest weights. Sector and thematic ETF flows are watched as a positioning gauge, showing where retail and institutional money is rotating. End-of-day rebalancing by leveraged ETFs can amplify late-session moves in trending markets. Flow data is descriptive rather than predictive, but it explains persistent bids and offers that pure fundamentals cannot.
