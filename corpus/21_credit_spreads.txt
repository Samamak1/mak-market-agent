Credit Spreads as a Risk Barometer
A credit spread is the extra yield a corporate bond pays over a government bond of similar maturity, compensating investors for default risk. Spreads on investment-grade and high-yield debt widen when the market grows worried about the economy and tighten when confidence returns, often moving before equities acknowledge the same stress. Equity investors track high-yield spreads because credit markets fund the corporate sector; when financing gets expensive, buybacks, capital spending and refinancing all suffer. Sharply widening spreads alongside a calm stock market is a divergence that risk managers take seriously.
