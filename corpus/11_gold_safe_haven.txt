Gold as a Safe Haven
Gold pays no interest and generates no earnings, so its price is driven by real yields, the dollar, and fear. When inflation-adjusted bond yields fall, the opportunity cost of holding gold drops and its price tends to rise. Crisis episodes bring safe-haven bids as investors look for assets outside the banking system, though in the sharpest liquidations gold can fall too as traders sell whatever is liquid to raise cash. Central bank purchases have become a meaningful, persistent source of demand. Gold's role in a portfolio is usually diversification and tail protection rather than an expected-return engine.
