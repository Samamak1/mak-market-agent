Inflation Data and CPI Day
Consumer price index releases are among the most market-moving scheduled events because inflation drives central bank policy. Traders parse the headline number, the core reading that strips food and energy, and the month-over-month trend, comparing each against consensus forecasts. A hotter-than-expected print pushes yields higher and typically pressures equities as markets price in tighter policy, while a cool print can spark rallies in both bonds and stocks. The first minutes after the release are often violent and illiquid, with initial moves sometimes fully reversing as the details are digested. Shelter costs, wage-sensitive services and energy base effects are the components analysts argue about most.
