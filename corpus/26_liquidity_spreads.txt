Liquidity, Spreads and Slippage
Liquidity is the ability to trade size without moving the price, and it varies enormously across names and across hours of the day. The bid-ask spread is the most visible cost: tight spreads on heavily traded stocks make in-and-out trading cheap, while wide spreads on small names mean a position starts underwater. Depth matters as much as the spread, since a large order walks through multiple price levels, creating slippage between the intended and achieved price. Liquidity evaporates exactly when it is needed most, during shocks, which is why position sizing should assume worse liquidity than today's screen shows.
