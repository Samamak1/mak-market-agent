Small Caps versus Large Caps
Small-capitalization stocks are more domestically focused, more dependent on external financing, and more sensitive to rates and credit conditions than the multinational giants that dominate large-cap indexes. They tend to lead early in economic recoveries, when risk appetite and credit availability improve, and lag when financing tightens. The ratio of a small-cap index to a large-cap index is a common risk-appetite gauge. Because a handful of mega-cap names can drive the headline index, small caps often give a cleaner read on the average company. Liquidity is thinner, so moves in both directions run further.
