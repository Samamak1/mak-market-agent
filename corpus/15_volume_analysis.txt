What Volume Confirms
Volume measures how many shares or contracts changed hands and serves as the conviction gauge behind a price move. A breakout on heavy volume suggests genuine repricing with broad participation, while the same breakout on thin volume is easier to fade. Climactic volume after an extended trend can mark exhaustion, as the last buyers or sellers rush in. Comparing today's volume to its recent average matters more than raw totals, since baseline activity varies by name and by season. Volume analysis is confirmation, not prophecy: it grades the quality of moves that price has already made.
