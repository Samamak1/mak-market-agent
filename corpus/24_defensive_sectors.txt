Defensive Sectors in Drawdowns
Utilities, consumer staples and healthcare are labeled defensive because demand for electricity, groceries and medicine holds up regardless of the economy. In selloffs these sectors typically fall less than the index, and their relative outperformance is itself a signal that investors are positioning for weakness. Defensives usually pay steadier dividends, which makes them rate sensitive: when bond yields rise, their yield advantage shrinks and they can underperform even in down markets. Rotation into defensives late in a rally is a classic caution flag. Defense in equities is relative, not absolute; defensive stocks still fall in bear markets.
