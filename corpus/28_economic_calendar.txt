Trading the Economic Calendar
Scheduled data releases, including monthly jobs reports, inflation prints, central bank decisions and purchasing manager surveys, are published at fixed times with consensus forecasts attached. The market reaction is driven by the surprise, the gap between the released figure and expectations, and by revisions to prior months, which are frequently overlooked. Liquidity thins in the minutes around a major release and spreads widen, so fills are worst exactly when moves are largest. Many traders flatten or reduce positions ahead of top-tier events, treating the release as an uncontrolled risk rather than an opportunity. Knowing the week's calendar is basic hygiene.
